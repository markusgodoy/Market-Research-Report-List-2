<p><p>Floating parquet floor refers to a type of flooring that is not nailed or glued down to the subfloor. There are two main types of floating parquet floors in the market: engineered and solid. Engineered floating parquet floors are made up of multiple layers of wood with a veneer top layer, providing stability and resistance to moisture. Solid floating parquet floors are made entirely of one type of wood, offering a traditional and authentic look. Both types offer durability, easy installation, and a wide range of design options for consumers.</p></p>
