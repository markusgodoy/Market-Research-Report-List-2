<p><p>Medical implantable defibrillators are devices used to treat life-threatening arrhythmias by delivering an electric shock to restore normal heart rhythm. There are two main types in the market: subcutaneous implantable cardioverter-defibrillators (S-ICDs) and transvenous implantable cardioverter-defibrillators (T-ICDs). S-ICDs are implanted just beneath the skin, while T-ICDs are placed in the chest cavity near the heart. Additionally, there are other types of implantable defibrillators available in the market, each offering unique features and benefits for patients.</p></p>
