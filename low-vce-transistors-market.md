<p><p>Low VCE transistors refer to transistors with low collector-emitter saturation voltage. There are two main types of low VCE transistors in the market: PNP and NPN. PNP transistors have a positive-negative-positive configuration, with the emitter being more positively charged than the collector. NPN transistors, on the other hand, have a negative-positive-negative configuration, with the collector being more positively charged than the emitter. Both types are commonly used in a variety of electronic devices for amplification and switching applications.</p></p>
