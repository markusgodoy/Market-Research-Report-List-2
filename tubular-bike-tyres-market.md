<p><p>Tubular bike tires, also known as sew-up tires, are a type of tire that is glued onto the rim of a tubular wheel. They are commonly used in professional road cycling due to their low rolling resistance and lightweight design. Tubular bike tires are known for providing a smoother ride and better handling compared to traditional clincher tires.</p><p>The tubular bike tires market is experiencing steady growth, with an expected CAGR of 6.3% during the forecast period. The increasing popularity of cycling as a recreational activity and the growing trend of biking as a sustainable mode of transportation are driving the demand for tubular bike tires. Additionally, advancements in tire technology, such as enhanced puncture resistance and improved grip, are also contributing to the market growth.</p><p>Manufacturers are focusing on developing innovative tubular bike tires to meet the evolving needs of cyclists. This includes designing tires with superior durability, reduced rolling resistance, and enhanced grip on various road surfaces. The introduction of tubeless tubular tires is also a trend that is gaining traction in the market, as they offer lower rolling resistance and improved puncture protection. Overall, the tubular bike tires market is expected to continue its growth trajectory in the coming years.</p></p>
