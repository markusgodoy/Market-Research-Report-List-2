<p><p>Animal protein glue is categorized into two main types - Black Type and Amber Type. Black Type glue is made from animal hides, primarily from cows, and is known for its strong bonding capabilities and water resistance. It is commonly used in woodworking and bookbinding industries. On the other hand, Amber Type glue is made from animal bones and is valued for its high adhesive strength and flexibility. It is commonly used in the production of musical instruments and restoration work. Both types of animal protein glue have their own unique properties and applications in various industries.</p></p>
