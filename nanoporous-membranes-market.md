<p><p>Nanoporous membranes are categorized into two main types: organic nanoporous membranes and inorganic nanoporous membranes. Organic nanoporous membranes are composed of organic polymers such as cellulose acetate or polycarbonate, offering high flexibility and compatibility with biological molecules. In contrast, inorganic nanoporous membranes are made from materials like ceramics or metals, providing excellent chemical and thermal stability. Both types of membranes are used in various applications, including water purification, gas separation, and drug delivery.</p></p>
