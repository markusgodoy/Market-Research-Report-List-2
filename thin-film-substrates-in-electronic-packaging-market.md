<p><p>Thin film substrates in electronic packaging refer to the material used to support and connect electronic components in devices. There are two main types of thin film substrates: rigid and flexible. Rigid thin-film substrates provide a stable base for components, while flexible thin-film substrates offer bendability and conformability to fit into tight spaces. Both types play a crucial role in modern electronic packaging, allowing for compact and efficient design of electronic devices.</p></p>
