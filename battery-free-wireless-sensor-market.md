<p><p>Battery-free wireless sensors are classified into three main market types based on their frequency: Low Frequency, High Frequency, and Ultra-High Frequency. Low frequency sensors operate at frequencies below 1 GHz, making them suitable for long-range communication. High frequency sensors operate at frequencies between 1 GHz and 6 GHz, offering higher data rates and shorter range. Ultra-high frequency sensors operate at frequencies above 6 GHz, providing high data rates and short-range communication. Each market type caters to different applications and requirements within the wireless sensor industry.</p></p>
