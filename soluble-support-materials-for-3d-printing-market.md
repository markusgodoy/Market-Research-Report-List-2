<p><p>Soluble support materials for 3D printing are used to create temporary structures that help print complex designs without the need for manual removal. Popular types of soluble support materials include PVA (polyvinyl alcohol) and HIPS (high-impact polystyrene). These materials dissolve easily in water or other solvents, leaving behind a clean finished product. In addition to PVA and HIPS, there are other materials available for specific applications, such as BVOH (butenediol vinyl alcohol co-polymer) and PETG (polyethylene terephthalate glycol). Each type of soluble support material offers unique properties and advantages for different 3D printing projects.</p></p>
