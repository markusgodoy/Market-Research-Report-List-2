<p><p>Emulsion stabilizers are substances used to maintain the stability and texture of emulsions in various products. Some common types include Xanthan Gum, Pectin, Carboxymethyl Cellulose, Gum Acacia, Starch, and others. Xanthan Gum is a natural thickening agent, Pectin is a plant-based stabilizer, Carboxymethyl Cellulose provides viscosity control, Gum Acacia is a natural gum with emulsifying properties, Starch acts as a thickener, and other stabilizers can include proteins, phospholipids, or synthetic polymers. Each type serves a specific purpose in maintaining the desired consistency of emulsions.</p></p>
