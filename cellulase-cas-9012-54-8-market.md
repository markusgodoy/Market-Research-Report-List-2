<p><p>Cellulase (CAS 9012-54-8) is an enzyme that breaks down cellulose into glucose, primarily used in industries such as food and beverage, textile, and biofuel production. There are three main types of cellulases in the market - Endoglucanase (EG), which attacks internal bonds of cellulose; Cellobiohydrolase (CBH), which acts on the non-reducing ends of cellulose chains; and Beta-Glucosidase (BG), which converts cellobiose into glucose. Each type of cellulase plays a specific role in the efficient breakdown of cellulose into usable sugars.</p></p>
