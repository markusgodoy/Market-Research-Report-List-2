<p><p>Swine feed phosphates market types include Monocalcium Phosphate (MCP), Dicalcium Phosphate (DCP), Mono-Dicalcium Phosphate (MDCP), and Tricalcium Phosphate (TCP). MCP is a common source of phosphorus and calcium in swine diets. DCP provides both phosphorus and calcium for swine growth. MDCP is a combination of MCP and DCP, offering balanced nutrition. TCP is primarily used as a calcium supplement in swine feed. Each type of phosphate plays a crucial role in promoting healthy growth and development in swine.</p></p>
