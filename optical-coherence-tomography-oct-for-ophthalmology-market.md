<p><p>Optical Coherence Tomography (OCT) is a non-invasive imaging technology used in ophthalmology to visualize the different layers of the retina. There are two main types of OCT systems on the market: Spectral Domain OCT and Swept Source OCT. Spectral Domain OCT provides high resolution images quickly, making it ideal for clinical settings. Swept Source OCT, on the other hand, offers deeper penetration into the tissue and is better suited for imaging of the choroid and optic nerve head. Both technologies play a crucial role in the diagnosis and management of various eye diseases.</p></p>
