<p><p>Hemodialysis (HD) membrane types include unmodified cellulose, modified/regenerated cellulose, and synthetic membranes. Unmodified cellulose membranes are derived from natural sources and offer biocompatibility. Modified/regenerated cellulose membranes are chemically treated to improve selectivity and biocompatibility. Synthetic membranes are created from man-made polymers and provide enhanced performance in terms of solute removal and biocompatibility. Each type of membrane has its own advantages and disadvantages, catering to different patient needs and preferences in the hemodialysis market.</p></p>
